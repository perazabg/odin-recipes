Tamales

About Dish

Tamales are a traditional Mexican dish consisting of prepared corn masa that is filled with delicious fillings and wrapped in corn husks or banana leaves before being steamed. They can be found in many different sizes and flavors

Prep Time: 30 minutes

Cook Time: 1 hour 30 minutes

Total Time: 2 hrs

Servings: 15 tamales

Ingredients

  • 30 Corn Husks for Tamales
Filling
  • 1 Russet Potato Diced
  • 2 Carrots Diced
  • 2 Chicken Breasts Cooked and Shredded
  • 1 tsp Ground Cumin
  • 1 tbsp Olive oil
  • 1/4 Onion julienned
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp pepper
Masa
  • 2.5 cups Masa for Tamales
  • 1.25 cups Chicken Broth
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 8 oz pork lard
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
Guajillo Salsa
  • 6 Guajillo Peppers deveined, deseeded, boiled, and cleaned
  • 2 Pasilla Peppers deveined, deseeded, boiled, and cleaned
  • 1/4 Onion roasted
  • 3 cloves Garlic roasted
  • 1/4 cup Chicken Broth
  • 1 tsp Cumin
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1 tsp pepper

Cooking Directions

  1. Start by soaking the corn husks in lukewarm water so that they get softer. Once soft, strain the water and set the husks aside.
  2. Fill the bottom part of the steamer with water and a little bit of salt, making sure that it doesn't reach the space where the tamales will be placed.
Masa
  1. Add all of the dry ingredients, including the lard, to a large bowl and mix well.
  2. Start adding the chicken broth a little at a time while stirring until a soft, malleable dough is formed
Filling
  1. Sautee the onion and chicken with the spices and olive oil, set aside.
  2. Add the carrots to boiling water for 5 minutes.
  3. Add the potatoes and let everything boil together for an additional 10 minutes. The potatoes and carrots should still be pretty firm.
  4. Transfer the carrots and potatoes to a frying pan and cook with salt and pepper over medium heat for a couple of minutes. Set aside,
Guajillo Salsa
  1. Combine all of the ingredients in a blender and blend well. If it is still a little bit thick, strain the mixture and only use the strained liquid.
Making and cooking the Tamales
  1. Take two of the corn husks and overlap one on top of the other, leaving the end separated
  2. Add 1/4 cup of the maza on top of the husks, then top with the chicken filling, the vegetables, and guajillo salsa.
  3. Fold over like a gift, putting the seam down.
  4. Add to the steamer, and cook for 1 - 1.5 hours.
